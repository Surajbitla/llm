Spider-Man is a fictional superhero appearing in Marvel Comics.
His real name is Peter Benjamin Parker.
Spider-Man was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Steve Ditko.
He first appeared in Amazing Fantasy #15 in August 1962.
Peter Parker is an ordinary teenager living in Queens, New York.
He gains superpowers after being bitten by a radioactive spider.
His powers include superhuman strength, agility, and the ability to cling to walls.
Spider-Man also has a "spider-sense" that warns him of danger.
Peter creates web-shooters to swing between buildings and trap enemies.
His iconic costume is red and blue with a black spider emblem.
Peter Parker lives with his Aunt May and Uncle Ben.
Uncle Ben's death inspires Peter's mantra: "With great power comes great responsibility."
Spider-Man balances his superhero duties with his everyday life.
Peter often struggles with school, work, and relationships.
He is one of the most relatable superheroes due to his personal challenges.
Spider-Man’s first love interest is Gwen Stacy.
Another important love interest is Mary Jane Watson, whom he eventually marries in the comics.
Peter works as a photographer for the Daily Bugle under J. Jonah Jameson.
Jameson often portrays Spider-Man as a menace to society in his editorials.
Spider-Man’s rogues' gallery includes iconic villains like Green Goblin, Doctor Octopus, and Venom.
Green Goblin, aka Norman Osborn, is one of Spider-Man’s greatest foes.
Doctor Octopus is a scientist with mechanical tentacles and high intellect.
Venom is an alien symbiote that becomes one of Spider-Man's fiercest adversaries.
Spider-Man is a founding member of the superhero team, the Avengers, in the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU).
Spider-Man has appeared in several live-action films, animated series, and video games.
Tobey Maguire first portrayed Spider-Man in Sam Raimi’s trilogy (2002–2007).
Andrew Garfield played Spider-Man in The Amazing Spider-Man films (2012 and 2014).
Tom Holland portrays Spider-Man in the MCU, debuting in Captain America: Civil War.
Spider-Man’s MCU solo films include Spider-Man: Homecoming, Far From Home, and No Way Home.
In the MCU, Peter Parker is mentored by Tony Stark (Iron Man).
Spider-Man plays a critical role in the fight against Thanos in Avengers: Endgame.
The animated film Spider-Man: Into the Spider-Verse introduced Miles Morales, another Spider-Man.
Miles Morales is a teenager who takes up the Spider-Man mantle after Peter Parker in his universe dies.
Spider-Man is known for his witty humor and sarcastic quips during battles.
Peter Parker is a scientific genius, often inventing his own gadgets and solutions.
Spider-Man’s character represents themes of responsibility, selflessness, and perseverance.
He frequently deals with moral dilemmas and the consequences of his choices.
Spider-Man is one of Marvel’s most popular and iconic characters.
He has been a member of other superhero teams, such as the Fantastic Four and the X-Men in crossover comics.
Spider-Man has appeared in numerous animated TV series, including the 1994 Spider-Man show.
His origin story is one of the most retold and influential in superhero history.
Spider-Man often faces personal losses, including the deaths of Gwen Stacy and Aunt May.
Peter's secret identity is a recurring source of tension in his life.
He is known for his optimism, even in the face of overwhelming odds.
Spider-Man’s black suit, introduced in the 1980s, is an iconic alternate costume.
The black suit eventually becomes the Venom symbiote.
Spider-Man’s web fluid is a scientific marvel that he develops himself.
Peter Parker’s intelligence rivals other Marvel geniuses like Tony Stark and Reed Richards.
Spider-Man has inspired countless spin-off comics, characters, and merchandise.
His enduring popularity cements his place as one of the greatest superheroes of all time.
