The Hulk is a fictional superhero appearing in Marvel Comics.
His real name is Dr. Robert Bruce Banner.
The Hulk was created by writer Stan Lee and artist Jack Kirby.
He first appeared in The Incredible Hulk #1 in May 1962.
Dr. Bruce Banner is a brilliant scientist specializing in gamma radiation.
Banner transforms into the Hulk after being exposed to a gamma bomb explosion.
The transformation occurs when Banner experiences extreme stress or anger.
The Hulk is known for his immense strength and durability.
His strength increases as he becomes angrier, making him nearly unstoppable.
The Hulk’s iconic catchphrase is, “Hulk smash!”
His original comic book design featured grey skin, but it was later changed to green.
Bruce Banner struggles to control the Hulk, creating tension in his life.
The Hulk represents Banner’s suppressed emotions and inner turmoil.
Banner’s dual identity is both a curse and a source of power.
The Hulk is often hunted by the military, led by General Thaddeus “Thunderbolt” Ross.
General Ross is also the father of Betty Ross, Bruce Banner’s love interest.
Betty Ross is a key character in Banner’s life, often helping to calm the Hulk.
The Hulk’s arch-nemesis is the Abomination, a gamma-powered creature similar to him.
Another major foe is the Leader, a super-intelligent villain exposed to gamma radiation.
The Hulk is a founding member of the superhero team known as the Avengers.
He fights alongside heroes like Iron Man, Thor, and Captain America.
The Hulk made his live-action debut in the 1970s TV series The Incredible Hulk.
In the Marvel Cinematic Universe (MCU), he is portrayed by Edward Norton and later by Mark Ruffalo.
The MCU reintroduced the Hulk in The Incredible Hulk (2008), starring Edward Norton.
Mark Ruffalo debuted as the Hulk in The Avengers (2012).
In the MCU, the Hulk plays a crucial role in defeating Loki, Ultron, and Thanos.
The Hulk’s alter ego, Bruce Banner, is a scientific genius on par with Tony Stark and Reed Richards.
Banner develops technology to suppress the Hulk, such as the Hulkbuster armor with Tony Stark.
In Avengers: Endgame, Banner merges his intellect with the Hulk’s strength, becoming “Professor Hulk.”
Professor Hulk combines Banner’s brain and Hulk’s brawn for a balanced personality.
The Hulk has several iconic transformations, including Grey Hulk and World Breaker Hulk.
Grey Hulk is a more cunning and manipulative version of the Hulk.
World Breaker Hulk is one of the most powerful versions, capable of destroying planets.
The Hulk has starred in numerous animated series and video games.
He is known for his tragic backstory and the struggle to live a normal life.
The Hulk’s powers include superhuman strength, regeneration, and near-invulnerability.
He can leap vast distances and generate powerful shockwaves by clapping his hands.
The Hulk’s gamma radiation exposure makes him resistant to many forms of damage.
Despite his rage, the Hulk often uses his power to protect the innocent.
Banner has tried multiple times to cure himself of the Hulk condition, often unsuccessfully.
The Hulk has fought many Marvel heroes, including Thor, Wolverine, and Spider-Man.
In the comic arc Planet Hulk, the Hulk is exiled to another planet and becomes a gladiator.
Planet Hulk leads to World War Hulk, where he returns to Earth seeking revenge.
The Hulk has a son, Skaar, who appears in the Planet Hulk storyline.
The Hulk’s character explores themes of anger, control, and identity.
Banner’s intellect and the Hulk’s power often make him a reluctant but vital hero.
The Hulk’s green form is one of the most recognizable superhero designs in pop culture.
His tragic nature makes him one of Marvel’s most complex and relatable characters.
The Hulk has been a member of several superhero teams, including the Defenders.
The character’s enduring popularity cements him as one of Marvel’s greatest superheroes.
